SDG 14: Life Below Water

“Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources for sustainable development.”
Source: United Nations (2015), Sustainable Development Goals

Quote

“No water, no life. No blue, no green.”
— Sylvia Earle, Mission Blue (2010)

Concept

This project presents a generative visual system that explores the gradual impact of plastic pollution on marine ecosystems. Fish are represented as simple blue geometric forms, while plastic appears as sharp triangular shapes that move through the space like ocean currents. Rather than illustrating immediate destruction, the work focuses on subtle and cumulative disruption. As plastic elements gather around fish, their forms begin to distort, indicating stress and imbalance within the environment. When pollution intensifies, irregular shapes emerge around the fish, symbolizing contamination and ecological instability. The system is driven by simple rules such as distance, quantity, and conditional interactions, allowing complex behaviours to emerge from minimal logic. This reflects how real-world environmental damage often develops over time rather than through sudden events.

Connection to SDG and Quote

Marine biologist Sylvia Earle explains that every breath we take and every drop of water we drink connects us to the ocean. However, plastic pollution continues to harm marine animals and their habitats. Protecting Life Below Water (SDG 14) means protecting these ecosystems and the species that live in them. Simple choices, such as reducing single-use plastic, can help preserve ocean health and support marine life.

Generative System

  • Built using p5.js
  • Changes on each page load
  • Uses randomness, distance, counting, and conditional logic
  • Simulates ecological imbalance through simple visual rules
